Shelling & Golden HourTides, beach walks, and evening light
🐚 Shelling conditionsGood
📷 Evening golden hour7:34 PM – 8:28 PM

Shell yeah! Good shelling conditions mean the low tide window is worth planning around. Look along the fresh wrack line and just below the last high-tide mark, especially on quieter stretches with less foot traffic. For the best odds, walk slowly within about an hour of low tide and check shell piles near inlets, jetties, and gently sloped sandbars. Today's cue: Low tide at 3:34 PM · EAS wind · 1–3 ft surf.

Best timingWithin about an hour before or after low tide.
Where to lookWrack lines, inlet edges, shell piles, and sandbar drop-offs.
Good findsOlive shells, whelk pieces, coquinas, sand dollars, and Scotch bonnet fragments.
