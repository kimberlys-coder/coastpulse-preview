🐾 Paws at the BeachLocal ordinances · verify before you go
Are dogs allowed at the beach?
Yes, but rules vary by town. Check each town before you go.
Beach-by-beach dog rules
⚠️Myrtle Beach (City)
Restricted area — dogs generally NOT allowed on the main beach strand May–Sep. Designated pet-friendly areas available off the main beach.
Leash required in all public areas. $1,087 fine for violations in the restricted zone. Check current ordinances before visiting.
✅North Myrtle Beach
Before 9 AM and after 6 PM (Memorial Day – Labor Day); all day rest of year
Leash required. Waste cleanup required. Confirm current rules with the city before visiting.
🚫Surfside Beach
NOT allowed on the beach May 15 – Sep 15. Allowed rest of year.
Strict enforcement during summer season. Leash required year-round when allowed.
✅Pawleys Island
Allowed year-round — no seasonal restrictions
Leash required at all times. Stay off protected dune vegetation. Waste cleanup required.
✅Huntington Beach State Park
Allowed on beach and in campground year-round
Leash required (6-ft max). Proof of current rabies vaccination required. Entry fee applies.
✅Litchfield Beach
Allowed year-round — no seasonal restrictions on public beach access
Leash required. Keep pets off dunes and sea oats.
Rules are subject to change. Always verify current ordinances with the town or city before visiting. Enforcement is often strict during summer season on the Grand Strand.
